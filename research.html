Discipline · Research

Understanding behavior
before building structure

This work identifies what people are trying to do, where they hesitate, and what they need to understand before they can act. Interviews, heuristic analysis, and synthesis all point toward the same goal: changing systems so they feel easier to enter and easier to trust.

See Case Study View Strategy
Research Position
Clarity starts before layout. It starts with what users are uncertain about.

The strongest insight is often not "users need more content." It is "users need the system to explain itself differently."

Focus

What the research is
solving for

Research here works as a decision tool. It reveals when category labels, flow order, or explanation patterns are asking users to do too much interpretive work.

Users

Who is arriving

Different visitors start with different levels of certainty. Good research distinguishes browsing, planning, and action instead of treating everyone as the same user.

Questions

What they need answered

The key issue is often not missing information but missing confidence. Users need to know what something is, what it does, and why it might fit them.

Outcome

What changes next

Findings translate into better sequence, better labels, stronger grouping, and earlier explanation where confusion would otherwise block movement.

Patterns

What repeatedly
shows up

Across projects, the same themes recur: ambiguous naming, fragmented pathways, information arriving too late, and interfaces organized around internal logic instead of user intent.

Behavioral patterns

People often arrive with a purpose but without the language to describe it. They compare options through mood, risk, effort, or expected outcome, while the interface presents business categories.

Research makes that mismatch visible so structure can be reorganized around how people actually decide.

Design implications

The answer is rarely just more copy. It is usually a change in sequence, naming, or hierarchy so meaning appears before commitment is required.

That is why research here is tightly connected to writing, strategy, and design rather than ending as a standalone report.
