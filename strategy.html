Function

What strategy is doing
inside the work

Strategy is the translation layer. It converts research into direction, direction into priorities, and priorities into decisions about what gets built and in what order.

Definition

What the product is for

Before structure, copy, or design, strategy defines the job of the product and what kind of person it is built to help. That definition shapes everything downstream.

Prioritization

What comes first

A product can't do everything equally well. Strategy identifies which decisions matter most â the ones users encounter first and the ones that cause the most friction when wrong.

Alignment

Keeping disciplines connected

Research, writing, IA, and design can all be technically correct and still produce incoherent experiences. Strategy is what keeps them working toward the same outcome.

Process

get made

Good strategy work starts with what the research shows, not with what looks right. The goal is decisions that can be explained and defended at every stage.

Connection

How strategy connects

Strategy is not its own output â it is the reasoning that makes research findings, writing choices, and design decisions coherent as a system.

Research â Strategy

Research identifies what users need and where they struggle. Strategy converts those observations into decisions about what to build, change, or remove.

Without strategy, research findings produce recommendations instead of decisions. With it, they produce a clear direction the rest of the work can follow.

Strategy â Execution

Strategic decisions show up in IA, page copy, navigation structure, and visual hierarchy. Each execution choice should be traceable back to a goal.

That traceability is what distinguishes design that can be defended from design that merely looks good.
